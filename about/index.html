About Me

I entered the University of Cambridge in 2018 as an undergraduate. Now I am doing my PartIII course in Computer Science and expected to graduate in July 2022 with a Masters degree.

I started to develop Programmable Hanafuda, an independent game made almost entirely by myself starting from an empty repository in the summer of 2020. The motivation of doing so was to apply what I learned from the Compiler Construction course, and design a simple language that is capable of describing the standard rules (yakus) and any varients of the traditional Japanese card game, Hanafuda.

Later, the project grew into a minimalistic game engine (without a GUI) and it had the potential of becoming an indie game. I had to put it away for a while because I need to focus on the university’s course. But, I picked it up in the summer of 2021 and completed this game as shown in the demo page.

I enjoy being an independent developer, especially the feeling of being the engineer, the designer, the producer and the player at the same time. As a CompSci, I have high software-engineering skills and experience; meanwhile, I have a good aesthetic taste and I am familiar with tools needed for designing the user interface, preparing GUI resources and texures, and even sound production and composing. The best thing of being a versatile is finding a place where all of my skills are incredibly useful.

I am also into classical and rock music. I used to appear in lots of recitals and concerts around Cambridge and London as an audience, and I was the keyboardist in the Robinson college’s rock band.

Sadly, those activities had to cease last year because of the COVID-19. Luckily, I have a Roland FP-90 piano in my room that keeps me away from boredom and over-working.

Finally, For your interest, I was born and grew up in GuangXi province, China. I moved to Beijing later on and this is where I live now when I’m not at Cambridge.
